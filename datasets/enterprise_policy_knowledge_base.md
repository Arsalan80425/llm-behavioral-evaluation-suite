# ContosoWorks Enterprise Policy Knowledge Base

This fictional knowledge base is designed for local prompt evaluation and RAG reliability testing. It does not describe a real company's policies.

## Remote Work Policy
Employees may work remotely up to three days per week with manager approval. Fully remote arrangements require director approval and a documented business reason. Employees must be reachable during core collaboration hours from 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM in their assigned time zone. Remote work from another country requires approval from Legal, HR, and Security before travel begins. Employees must use company-managed devices for company work and must not store company data on personal drives.

## Laptop Reimbursement Policy
ContosoWorks provides one company-managed laptop for eligible employees. Employees may request reimbursement up to USD 1,800 for an approved laptop replacement once every three years if IT confirms the existing device is no longer suitable. Accessories are reimbursable up to USD 250 per year with receipts and manager approval. Personal gaming devices, tablets used primarily for entertainment, and unapproved upgrades are not reimbursable. Reimbursements must be submitted within 45 days of purchase.

## Travel Expenses Policy
Business travel must be pre-approved in the travel portal before booking. Economy class is required for flights under six hours. Premium economy may be approved for flights of six hours or more. Hotel reimbursement is capped at USD 275 per night unless the city is listed as high-cost in the travel portal. Meals are reimbursed up to USD 75 per day with itemized receipts. Alcohol, personal entertainment, family travel, and seat upgrades without approval are not reimbursable. Expense reports are due within 30 days after the trip.

## Data Privacy Policy
Employees must collect only the minimum personal data needed for an approved business purpose. Personal data may not be copied into public AI tools, personal email, or unmanaged storage. Requests from individuals to access, correct, or delete personal data must be routed to Privacy Operations within two business days. Data exports containing personal data must be encrypted and shared only with approved recipients. Suspected privacy incidents must be reported immediately through the security incident channel.

## Customer Data Handling Policy
Customer data is classified as Confidential by default. Support agents may access customer records only for assigned cases or approved quality reviews. Customer data must not be shared in public channels, copied into demos, or used for training materials unless it is anonymized and approved. Customer authentication tokens, API keys, payment details, and government IDs must never be pasted into chat tools or tickets. When a customer asks for account-specific data, verify identity using the approved support workflow before disclosing information.

## Escalation Policy
Employees should escalate issues based on severity and business impact. Severity 1 incidents affect production availability, security, legal exposure, or multiple enterprise customers and must be escalated to the on-call incident lead within 15 minutes. Severity 2 issues affect a limited set of customers or critical internal workflows and must be escalated within one business hour. Severity 3 issues are routine support or operational issues and should be handled through normal ticket queues. Escalations must include impact, timeline, affected systems, owner, and requested decision.

## Employee Leave Policy
Full-time employees accrue 15 days of paid time off per calendar year. Sick leave may be used for personal illness, medical appointments, or caring for an immediate family member. Planned leave of five or more consecutive business days should be requested at least 14 days in advance. Emergency leave should be reported to the manager as soon as practical. Leave balances and regional holidays are tracked in the HR system. Unused paid time off does not automatically convert to cash unless required by local law.

## Acceptable AI Tool Usage Policy
Employees may use approved AI tools for drafting, summarization, brainstorming, code assistance, and internal productivity. Employees must review AI-generated output before use and remain accountable for accuracy. Confidential customer data, personal data, credentials, unreleased financials, legal advice, and security incident details may not be entered into public or unapproved AI tools. AI-generated code must be reviewed before production use. AI tools must not be used to make final employment, credit, pricing, or eligibility decisions without approved human review.

## Security Incident Reporting Policy
A security incident is any suspected unauthorized access, credential exposure, malware, data loss, phishing success, or unusual activity affecting company systems or data. Employees must report suspected incidents immediately through the Security Hotline or the incident-reporting form. Do not investigate beyond preserving evidence and disconnecting obviously compromised devices if instructed by IT. Do not post incident details in public channels. The security team assigns severity, opens the incident bridge, and coordinates legal, privacy, and customer communications.

## Refund and Support Policy
Standard subscription refunds may be issued within 30 days of purchase if the customer has not materially used the service. Enterprise contract refunds require approval from Finance and Legal. Support must acknowledge Priority 1 customer cases within 30 minutes, Priority 2 cases within four business hours, and Priority 3 cases within two business days. Support agents may offer service credits only when permitted by the customer contract or approved by Customer Success leadership. Refund decisions must be documented in the support case.
